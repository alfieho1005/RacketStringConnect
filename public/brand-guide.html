01 — Brand Story
Why we exist

StringConnect exists solely to help players discover local stringing services and click through to the providers they trust.

Every page keeps contact external — WhatsApp, Instagram, email, or phone links immediately leave the platform so follow-up, pricing, and scheduling happen directly with the stringer. The experience is clean, friendly, and mobile-first.

No marketplace frictions. No internal messaging. No data capture beyond discovery. We celebrate the craft of stringing, surface trusted professionals — then get out of the way.

🔗

Pure Connection

Discovery-only. We connect, never intermediate.

↗

Contact External

All follow-up happens off-platform. No bookings or chat.

🎾

Craft First

Stringers are skilled professionals, not vendors.

"We surface trusted stringers and then get out of the way so you can connect directly."

— About page tagline

07 — Design Principles
Rules we build by
📱

Mobile-first, always

44px minimum tap targets. Horizontal scroll pills. Stacked layouts. Most users are on phones, browsing between sets.

⚡

Flat and direct

No glassmorphism, no gradient blobs. White cards, slate backgrounds, yellow accents. Every pixel earns its place.

🟡

Yellow as signal

Yellow marks every action worth taking. Used sparingly on CTAs, active states, and the logo so it retains meaning.

🚀

Discovery, then exit

Every contact button is an external link. The platform's job ends at introduction. Design for fast hand-off, not retention.

08 — Photography
How we shoot
← hero image · racketstringconnect.jpg
What to shoot ✓
Hands-on craft — fingers on strings, tools in use
Warm workshop lighting, natural and golden
Shallow depth of field — focus on the skill
Real stringers, real spaces (not staged sets)
Wide landscape crops — subject on one side, space for text
Close-ups: string grid patterns, tension clamps, reels
What to avoid ✕
Generic stock sport photos (players mid-swing)
Cold or clinical studio lighting
Brand logos prominently visible on rackets
Over-edited, hyper-saturated colours
Portrait orientation — hero needs landscape
Empty courts or stadiums — we're about the craft, not the game
🌡️

Warm tones

Ambers, browns, soft yellows. Photography is warm — UI stays flat and cool.

✂️

Tight crop

Crop in close. The string grid and hands are the story — not the full room.

📐

16:9 ratio

Hero images should be landscape 16:9 or wider. Minimum 1200px wide.

Note: photography can be cinematic and warm — the UI stays flat. They complement, not copy each other.
